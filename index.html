Simulation Math & Mapping Rules

Understanding consecutive-digit addition logic

The core rule

When you input or upload numbers (e.g. 23892), the engine picks the first two numbers, adds them together, and finds the action corresponding to their sum:

  • 2 and 3 added together = 5 (which corresponds to a Dot Ball).
  • Then, 8 and 9 added together = 17 (which corresponds to a Wicket).
  • The last digit 2 remains in the queue. When you input the next set of numbers, 2 is automatically combined with the first digit of your new input!

Roster Attributes & Traits

Tactics and player ratings influence the simulation calculations:

  • Aggressive Batsmen: Heighten boundary frequency but amplify catch/bowled risks on critical sums.
  • Defensive Batsmen: Safely convert wicket-prone sums into block dot balls based on high rating scores.
  • Ratings: Higher Bowler Ratings can increase bowler-friendly wickets, and higher Batsman Ratings trigger clutch runs!
